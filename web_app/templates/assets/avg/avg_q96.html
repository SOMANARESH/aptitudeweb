Question :96

In an examination, the average marks of all the students calculated to be 58 marks. It is later found that marks of 60 students were wrongly written as 70 instead of 50. If the corrected average is 55, find the total number of candidates who took the exam.

A.  100

B.  250

C.  400

D.  500

Answer: C

Let us assume that the number of students be X

The average of X students is 58

(Sum of marks by X students)⁄X = 58

Sum of X students marks = 58X

Now given that the Actual average of marks by 60 students is 50

(Actual Sum of marks by 60 students)⁄60 = 50

Actual Sum of marks by 60 students = 60×50

Actual Sum of marks by 60 students = 3000

But by mistake average marks is written as 70 instead of 50

So The wrong average of marks by 60 students is 70

(Wrong Sum of marks by 60 students)⁄60 = 70

Actual Sum of marks by 60 students = 60×70

Actual Sum of marks by 60 students = 4200

Now total change in marks = 4200-3000

Now total change in marks = 1200

Given thet the corrected average after removing marks by wrong average is 55

(Corrected Sum of marks by X students)⁄X = 55

Corrected Sum of marks by X students = 55X

Sum of X students-1200 = 55X

58X-1200 = 55X

58X-55X = 1200

3x = 1200

x = 400
