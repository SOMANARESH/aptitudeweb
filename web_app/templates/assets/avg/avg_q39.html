Question :39

The average of 5 consecutive integers starting with m is n. What is the average of 6 consecutive integers starting with (m+2)

A.  (n+3)

B.   (2n+9)⁄2

C.   (2n+5)⁄2

D.  (n+2)

Answer: C

Let us assume that the 5 consecutive numbers be m,m+1,m+2,m+3,m+4 Given that the average of 5 consecutive numbers start with m is n

(Sum of 5 consecutive numbers start with m)⁄5

= n

m+m+1+m+2+m+3+m+4 = 5n

5m+10 = 5n

m+2 = n

m = n-2

Let us assume that m+2,m+3,m+4,m+5,m+6,m+7 be 6 consecutive numbers

Average of 6 consecutive integers start with (m + 2 )

= (Sum of 6 consecutive numbers start with m+2)⁄6

= (m+2+m+3+m+4+m+5+m+6+m+7)⁄6

= (6m+27)⁄6

= (6(n-2)+27)⁄6

= (6n-12+27)⁄6

= (6n+15)⁄6

= 3(2n+5)⁄6

= 2n+5⁄2
