Question :25

The average of 5 consecutive numbers is n. if the next two numbers are also included, the average of the 7 numbers will

A.  Increased by 2

B.  Increase by 1

C.  Remains the same

D.  Increase by 1.4


Let us assume that the five consecutive numbers be

m,m+1,m+2,m+3,m+4

Average of 5 numbers is n

i.e. (Sum of 5 consecutive numbers)⁄5 = n

(m+m+1+m+2+m+3+m+4)⁄5 = n

5m+10 = 5n

m+2 = n

m = n-2

The next two consecutive numbers are m+5,m+6

Then Average of 7 numbers is

= (Sum of 7 consecutive numbers) ⁄7

= (m+m+1+m+2+m+3+m+4+m+5+m+6)⁄7

= (7m+21)⁄7

= m+3

= n-2+3

= n+1

So average is increased by 1.
