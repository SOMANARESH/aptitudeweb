Question :77

The batting average for 40 innings of a cricket player is 50 runs. His highest score exceeds his lowest score by 172 runs. If these two innings are not included, the average of the remaining 38 innings is 48 runs. The highest score of the player is:

A.  165

B.  170

C.  172

D.  174

Answer: D

Let the lowest score be X

Then According to problem Highest score exceeds lowest by 172

i.e.Highest score = X+172

Given that the average runs by 40 innings is 50

Then (Sum of 40 innings score)⁄40 = 50

Sum of 40 innings score = 40×50

Sum of 40 innings score = 2000

Sum of 38 innings score + Lowest score+highest score = 2000

Sum of 38 innings score + X+X+172 = 2000

Sum of 38 innings score + 2X= 2000-172

Sum of 38 innings score + 2X= 1828--->(1)

Given that the average runs by 38 innings is 48

Then (Sum of 38 innings score)⁄38 = 48

Sum of 38 innings score = 38×48

Sum of 38 innings score = 1824

From(1)

1824+2X = 1828

2X = 4

X = 2

Highest score = X+172

Highest score = 2+172

Highest score = 174
