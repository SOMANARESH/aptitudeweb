The average age of 30 boys in a class is 15 years. One boy aged 20 years left the class, but two new boys came in his place whose ages differ by 5 years. If the average age of all the boys now in the class still remains 15 years, the age of the younger new comer is:

A.  20

B.  15

C.  10

D.  8

Answer: B

Given that average age of 30 boys is 15

(Sum of ages of 30 boys)⁄30 = 15

Sum of ages of 30 boys = 30×15

Sum of ages of 29 boys+20 = 450

Sum of ages of 29 boys = 450-20 = 430

Given that the average remains same when one boy of 20years replaced by two boys whose ages differ by 2 years

Let the average of one boy is X

Other boy age = X+5

Now average age becomes 18

Sum of ages of 29 boys+Sum of ages of two new boys = 31×15
