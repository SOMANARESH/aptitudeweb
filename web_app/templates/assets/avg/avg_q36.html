Question :36

If average of 5 observations is 46 and that of 5 other observations is 50, find the average of all the 10 observations.

A.  96

B.  50

C.  48

D.  46

Answer: C

Given that the average of first 5 observations is 46

(sum of first 5 observations)⁄5 = 46

Sum of first 5 observations = 5×46

Sum of first 5 observations = 230

And also given that the average of another group of 5 observations is 50

(sum of other 5 observations)⁄5 = 50

sum of other 5 observations = 5×50

sum of other 5 observations = 250

Now the average of these 10 observations is

= (sum of 10 observations)⁄10

= (230+250)⁄10

= 480⁄10

= 48
