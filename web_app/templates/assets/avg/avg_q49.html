Question :49

The average weight of three men A, B and C is 84kg. D joins them and the average weight of the four becomes 80 kg. if E whose weight is 3 kg more that D, replaces A, the average weight of B, C, D and E becomes 79kg. the weight of A is:

A.  65kg

B.  70 kg

C.  75 kg

D.  80 kg


Given that the average weight of A,B and C is 84

(Sum of weights of A,B and C)⁄3 = 84

A+B+C = 3×84 = 252--->(1)

Given that the average weight of A,B,C and D is 80

(Sum of weights of A,B,C and D)⁄4 = 80

A+B+C+D = 4×80 = 320--->(2)

Now let us calculate (2)-(1)

(A+B+C+D)-(A+B+C) = 320-252

D = 68

Given that Es weight is 3 more than that of D

E = D+3

E = 68+3

E = 71

And also The average weight of B,C,D and E is 79

(Sum of weights of B,C,D and E)⁄4 = 79

B+C+D+E = 4×79

B+C+68+71 = 316

B+C = 316-139

B+C = 177--->(3)

From (1)&(3)

A+B+C = 252

A+177 = 252

A = 252-177

A = 75
