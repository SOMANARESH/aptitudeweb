Question :32

Sum and average of some numbers are 400 and 40 respectively. If two numbers with average of 100 are also included, find new average of all the numbers.

A.  50

B.  60

C.  70

D.  data insufficient


Given sum and average of n numbers is 400 and 40 respectively.

Let us assume that X1,X2, X3....Xn be n numbers

Then X1+X2+X3....+Xn = 400

Average of n numbers is 40

i.e. Sum of n numbers⁄n = 40

(X1+X2+X3....+Xn)⁄n = 40

400 = n×40

n = 400⁄40

n = 10

Given two numbers of their average 200 is also included to n numbers.

Let Xn+1, Xn+2 be extra numbers

Given that the average of Xn+1, Xn+2 is 100

(Sum of Xn+1, Xn+2)⁄2 = 100

Xn+1+Xn+2 = 2×100 = 200

Now we need to calculate n+2 numbers average which is given by

= Sum of n+2 numbers⁄n+2

= (X1+X2+X3....+Xn+Xn+1+Xn+2)⁄(10+2)

= (400+200)⁄12

= 600⁄12

= 50
