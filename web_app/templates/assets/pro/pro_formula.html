Formulae on Profit and Loss


  1. A shopkeeper marks his goods at x% above the cost price and then allows y% discount on the marked price, then his profit percent.

    =  x - y - ( xy ⁄ 100 ).


  2. If price of an article is marked x% above the cost price and x% discount is allowed on its marked price, then loss percent.
    =   x2 ⁄ 100.


  3. If two articles are sold for the same selling price. On selling first article a man gains x% and on selling the other article he loses x%, in such cases there will be always loss, and
    Loss % = x2 ⁄ 100  
    i.e. (Common Gain and Loss %)2 ⁄ 100.


  4. If two articles are sold for the same selling price. On selling the first, the gain is x% and on selling the second the gain is y%, then % profit in the transaction

    =   100( x + y ) + 2xy ⁄ 200 + x + y


  5. Relation between profit/loss and cost price/selling price:

    When the article is sold at profit:

    We know that Cost Price + Profit = Selling Price.

    Cost price < selling Price.

    If we shift base from cost price to selling price( i.e to bigger base), smaller fraction is required and vice-versa

    (a). if profit is 1⁄x of cost price, then profit is 1⁄x+1 of the selling price.

    (b) if profit is 1⁄x of selling price, then profit is 1⁄x - 1 of the cost price.

    When the article is sold at Loss:

    We know that Cost Price - Loss = Selling Price.

    Cost price > selling Price.

    If we shift base from cost price to selling price( i.e to smaller base), bigger fraction is required and vice-versa.

    (a) If Loss is 1⁄x of cost price, then loss is 1⁄x - 1 of the selling price.

    (b) If loss is 1⁄x of selling price, then loss is 1⁄x + 1 of the cost price.


  6. on selling an article for x, a person earns a% profit. In order to earn b% profit, he must sell the article for

    =   x × 100 + b ⁄ 100 + a.
    i.e. new selling price = old selling price × a⁄b
    Where
    a = 100 + Desired Profit (in %)
    b = 100 + initial Profit (in %).


  7. if two articles are sold - first article at x% profit and the second article is at y% loss, thus making no profit, no loss in the transaction.

    Then cost price of the two articles are in the ratio y:x.
