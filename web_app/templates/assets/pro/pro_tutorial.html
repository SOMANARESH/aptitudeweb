Profit and Loss

Cost price

  • The price at which the article is purchased is called its cost price.

Selling price

  • The price at which the article is sold by is called selling price of the article.

Marked price

  • The price marked on the goods. Also called list price or cast price.

    • Selling price = marked price - discount.

Profit

  • An article is said to be sold at profit, if its selling price is more than its cost price.

    • Profit = selling price − cost price.
    Selling price = cost price + profit.

Loss

  • An article is said to be sold at loss if its selling price is less than its cost price.

    • Loss = cost price − selling price.
    Selling price = cost price - loss.

Profit and Loss Percent:

  • Profit/Loss percent is relation between Profit/Loss and cost price of an article. Therefore, profit or loss percent is always calculated on cost price, unless mentioned otherwise.

    Formula: % Profit = Profit ⁄ Cost price × 100.

    % Loss = Loss ⁄ Cost price × 100.

    Ex:  
    Question: An article costing 10 is sold for a profit of 2 Rs. Find profit percent.
    Sol: 2 ⁄ 10 × 100 = 20 %.

Margin

  • Profit as a percentage of selling price.

Relation between selling price and cost price:

  • When the article is sold at profit:
    Selling Price = Cost Price × 100 + % Profit ⁄ 100 .

    Cost Price = Selling Price × 100 ⁄ 100 + % Profit .

  • When the article is sold at Loss:
    Selling Price = Cost Price × 100 - % Loss ⁄ 100 .

    Cost Price = Selling Price × 100 ⁄ 100 - % Profit .

    Ex:  
    An article costing Rs-400, is sold at 20% profit. Find its selling price.

    Sol: Selling Price = 400 × 100 + 20 ⁄ 100
    = 400 × 120 ⁄ 100
    = Rs 480.

Calculating profit percent when gain in articles is given:

  • If on selling x article, gain is equal to the cost price of y articles, then
    % Profit =   y ⁄ x × 100.

  • If on selling x articles, gain is equal to the selling price of y articles,
    % Profit =   y⁄x - y × 100.

  • If cost price of x articles = selling price of y articles.
    % Profit =   x - y ⁄ y × 100.

  • Loss is denoted by negative point.
