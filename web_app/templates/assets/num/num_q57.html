A two digit number is three times he sum of its digits. If 45 is added to the number, its digits are reversed. The sum of digits of the number is:

A.  5

B.  7

C.  9

D.  11

Answer: C

Let the number is 10X+Y

Then 10X+Y = 3(X+Y)

7X-2Y = 0

7X = 2Y − − − − −>(1)

On adding 45 to the number, its digits are reversed.

Difference between x and y is 45÷9 = 5 and X < Y

Y = X + 5− − − − −>(2)

Putting this value in (1), we get 7X = 2(X+5)

X = 2 , Y = 7

The number is 27 and the sum of the digits is 2+7=9.
