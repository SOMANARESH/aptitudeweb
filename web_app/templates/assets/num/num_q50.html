The sum of the digits of two digit number is 13. If 9 is added to the number, the digits are reversed. What is the number?

A.  36

B.  67

C.  76

D.  116

Answer: b

Let the two digit number be XY then it can be represented in decimal format as below

(101×X)+(100×Y)--->1

By Reversing the digits of the number we get YX and it can be represented in decimal form as

(101×Y)+(100×X)--->2

Given that the sum of the digits of a number is 13

i.e. X+Y = 13--->3

If 9 is added to equation 1 then we get euqtion 2

i.e. (101×X)+(100×Y)+9 = (101×Y)+(100×X)

10X+Y +9 = 10Y+X

9Y-9X = 9

9(Y-X) = 9

Y-X = 1--->4

By solving eq.3 and eq.4 we will get

2Y = 14

Y = 7

From eq.4

Y-X = 1

X = Y-1

X = 7-1

X = 6

So the number is XY = 67
