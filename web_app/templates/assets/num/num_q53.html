The sum of the digits of a two digit number is 12. On adding 36 to the number, its digits are reversed; what is the number?

A.  39

B.  48

C.  57

D.  75


Let the two digit number be XY then it can be represented in decimal format as below

(101×X)+(100×Y)--->1

By Reversing the digits of the number we get YX and it can be represented in decimal form as

(101×Y)+(100×X)--->2

Given that the sum of the digits of a number is 12

i.e. X+Y = 12--->3

If 36 is added to equation 1 then we get euqtion 2

i.e. (101×X)+(100×Y)+36 = (101×Y)+(100×X)

10X+Y +36 = 10Y+X

9Y-9X = 36

9(Y-X) = 36

Y-X = 4--->4

By solving eq.3 and eq.4 we will get

2Y = 16

Y = 8

From eq.4

Y-X = 4

X = Y-4

X = 8-4

X = 4

So the number is XY = 48
