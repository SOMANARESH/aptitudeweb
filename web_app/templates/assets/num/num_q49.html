Question :49

The sum of digits of a two digit number is 8 and the difference between the number and that formed by reversing the digits is 18. What is the difference between the digits of the number?

A.  1

B.  2

C.  3

D.  4

Answer: b

Let the two digit number be XY then it can be represented in decimal format as below

(101×X)+(100×Y)--->1

By Reversing the digits of the number we get YX and it can be represented in decimal form as

(101×Y)+(100×X)--->2

Given that the sum of the digits of a number is 8

i.e. X+Y = 8

The difference between 1 and 2 is 18

i.e. (10X+Y)-(10Y+X) = 18

9X-9Y = 18

9(X-Y) = 18

X-Y = 2
