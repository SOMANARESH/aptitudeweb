The sum of the digits of a two digit number is 15. On subtracting 27 from the given number its digits are reversed. Find the number

A.  69

B.  78

C.  87

D.  96


Let the two digit number be XY then it can be represented in decimal format as below

(101×X)+(100×Y)--->1

By Reversing the digits of the number we get YX and it can be represented in decimal form as

(101×Y)+(100×X)--->2

Given that the sum of the digits of a number is 15

i.e. X+Y = 15--->3

If 27 is Subtracted from equation 1 then we get euqtion 2

i.e. (101×X)+(100×Y)-27 = (101×Y)+(100×X)

10X+Y -27 = 10Y+X

9X-9Y = 27

9(X-Y) = 27

X-Y = 3--->4

By solving eq.3 and eq.4 we will get

2X = 18

X = 9

From eq.4

X-Y = 3

Y= X-3

Y = 9-3

Y = 6

So the number is XY = 96
