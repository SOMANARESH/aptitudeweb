A number consists of two digits such that the digit in the tens place is less by 2 than the digit in the units place. Three times the number added to 6⁄7 times the number obtained by reversing the digits equals 108. The sum of digits in the number is:

A.  8

B.  9

C.  6

D.  7


Let the two digit number be XY then it can be represented in decimal format as below

(101×X)+(100×Y)--->1

By Reversing the digits of the number we get YX and it can be represented in decimal form as

(101×Y)+(100×X)--->2

Given that the digit in tens place is less by 2 than the digit in units place of the number

i.e. X = Y-2
