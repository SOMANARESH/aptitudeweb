The weight of a container full of water is 28kg. When I is 1⁄4 full, it weighs 19kg. its weight, when it is 2⁄3 full of water, will be:

A.  8 kg

B.  20 kg

C.  24 kg

D.  18.67 kg

Answer: C

Weight of container + full water = 28 kg −−−−−>(1)

And weight of container + 1⁄4 water = 19 kg−−−−−>(2)

(1)-(2): weight of 3⁄4 water = (28-19)kg = 9 kg

Weight of full water = 9×(4⁄3)=12 kg

Weight of empty container = 28 kg -12 kg = 16 kg

Weight of 2⁄3 water= (2⁄3)×12 = 8 kg

Required weight = 16+8

= 24.
