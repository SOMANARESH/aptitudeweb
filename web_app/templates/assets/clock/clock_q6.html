Question 6 :

A clock is set right at 8 a.m. The clock gains 10 minutes in 24 hours. What will be the true time when the clock indicates 1 p.m. on the following day?

A.1 Hr 12min

B.12 Hr 48min

C.12 Hr 42 min

D.1 Hr 18min

Answer: B

Solution:

Given that there is difference of 29 hours of between 8 O’clock AM and 1 O’clock PM

We also know that, Clock gains 10 minutes for 24 hours.

So that means 24 hours of correct clock = 24 hours 10 minutes of current clock

So 24 Hr 10 mins of Current Clock= 24 Hr of Correct clock

We know that 60 minutes = 1 Hr

So 10 minutes = (1⁄60) ×10

10 minutes = (1⁄6)

So (24+ (1⁄6))Hr <‐‐‐> 24 hr

[(24*6)+1]⁄6 Hr <‐‐‐> 24 Hr

145⁄6 Hr <‐‐‐> 24 Hr

1 Hr of current clock = 24 × (6⁄145) Hr of Correct clock

Then 29 Hrs of Current Clock = (24 × (6⁄145)×29) Hr of Correct clock

29 Hrs of Current Clock = 28.8 Hr

29 Hrs of Current Clock = 28 Hr + 0.8 Hr

29 Hrs of Current Clock = 28 Hr + (0.8×60)

29 Hrs of Current Clock = 28 Hr + 48 min of Correct clock

1 PM of Current Clock indicates 12 Hr 48 min PM
