Clock

Important Points

  • A clock is an instrument used for indicating and maintaining the time. It is an electronic device that present the duration of hour, minute and second

  • A clock has two hands. The smaller one is called Hour Hand or Short Hand where as larger one is called Minute Hand or Long Hand

  • The Face of a Clock represents a circle of an angle 360°

    • The minute is a unit of time equal to (1 ⁄60)th of an hour.

    • 1 hour = 60 mins

  • In Every hour

    • Hour’s hand and Minute’s Hand will coincide once.In this situation, the angle between these two is 0°

    • Both hands will opposite to each other once.At this point the angle between them are 180°

    • Both hands will be perpendicular to each other. At this point the angle between them is 90°

  • Calculations

    • In 60 minutes,minute hand will cover 360°

      So in 1 minute, minute hand will cover 360°⁄60 = 6°

    • In 12 Hours, Hour hand will cover 360°

      In 1 hour,hour hand will cover 360°⁄12 = 30°

      In 60 minutes(1 hour),hour hand will cover 30°

      In 1 minute,hour hand will cover 30°⁄60 = (1⁄2)°

    • So in 1 minute, minute hand cover (6°−(1⁄2)°) = (11⁄2)° more than the hour hand

      Minute hand gains (11⁄2)° over the hour hand

      So in 1 hour(60 minutes), minute hand gains 55minutes than the hour hand

  • Occurrences
    • Coincide together

      • The difference between minute hand and hour hand = 0°

      • In 1 hour,Both hands will coincide each other 1 time

      • In 12 hours(Half Day),both hands will coincide each 11 times (We have same occurrence at 12°O Clock two times in 12 hours of duration)

      • In 24 hours(Full Day),both hands will coincide each 22 times (We have same occurrence at 12°O Clock four times in 24 hours of duration)

    • Opposite to each other

      • The angle between minute and hour hand is 180° and time difference = 30minutes

    • Perpendicular to each other(Right Angle)

      • The difference between minute hand and hour hand is 15minutes or 90°

  • Note: Angle is measured with reference to 12
