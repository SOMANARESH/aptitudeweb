RATIO, PROPORTION AND PARTNERSHIP.

  • Ratio between two numbers x and y is expressed as x:y. it can also be expressed as x⁄y. The first term in a ratio is called antecedent and the second term is called consequent. Ratio has no unit.

  • Ratio between Rs:300 and Rs:500 is 3:5, not Rs:3 and Rs:5. There is no ratio between 5 cows and Rs:100. Ratio between two quantities is always expressed in the same unit.
Note:
  • Multiplication or division of each term of a ratio by the same non zero number does not effect the ratio. But the same is not true for Addition or Subtraction.

    = x : y = ax :ay = x ⁄ a : y ⁄ b, where a ≠ 0. But x : y ≠ (x + or - a) : (y + or - a)

Proportion:

  • The equality of two ratios is called proportion.

  • a, b, c and d are in proportion, if a:b = c: d. it can be expressed as a:b::c:d.

  • In any proportion, the first and forth proportional are called the extreme terms while the second and the third proportional are called mean terms.

  • In a proportion, product of Extremes = Product of Means i.e. a × d = b × c.

Inverse or Reciprocal Ratio:

  • If ratio between two numbers is a:b. Then the inverse ratio is

    = 1⁄a : 1⁄b or b : a.

    Ex: Inverse ratio of 2:3 is 3:2.

Direct Proportion:

  • Two quantities are said to be in direct proportion if on increase(or decrease ) in one quantity, the other quantity also increases (or decreases) proportionately i.e. in the same ratio.

    Ex:
    Number of articles bought and total cost of articles i.e. more articles, more cost and less articles, less cost. Cost per article and total cost of certain number of articles.

Inverse Proportion:

  • Two quantities are said to be in indirect or inverse proportion, if on increase in one quantity, the other quantity decreases in the same ratio and vice versa.

    Note:
    If ratio of prices of two commodities change in the ratio x:y, then with the same amount of money quantity can be purchased changes y:x.

Continued Proportion:

  • Three numbers are said to be in continued proportion when the ratio of the first number to the second is equal to ratio of the second number to the third. x, y and z are said to be in proportion if x : y :: y : z.

  • If x is third proportion of a and b.

    a : b = b : x then x = b 2⁄a.

Forth Proportion:

  • If x is Fourth Proportion of a, b and c.

    Then a : b = c : x then x = c × b⁄a.
