Question :14

5 years ago, the fathers age was 3 times of his sons age. 5 years hence the ratio between the ages of father and the son becomes 11:5. Find the fathers present age?

A.  40 years

B.  45 years

C.  50 years

D.  55 years


Let us assume that the present ages of Father and Son be F and S respectively

Given the fathers age is 3 times that of his Sons age before 5 years

(F-5) = 3×(S-5)

F = (3×S)-10--->1

And also Given that the ratio between fathers and his sons age becomes 11:5 after 5 years

i.e. F+5⁄S+5= 11⁄5

(5×F)+25 = (11×S)+55

Substitute 1 in Above equation we get

5×((3×S)-10) = (11×S)+30

(15×S)-50 = (11×S)+30

4×S = 80

=> S = 20

From Equation 1

F = (3×S)-10

F = 3×20-10

F = 60-10

F = 50.
