Question :33

A and B are 3 years and 2 years old respectively. Their father is 40 yeasrs. After how many years, fathers age would be twice of combined age of A and B?

A.  5 years

B.  10 years

C.  15 years

D.  20 years


Let us assume that the ages of Father and his two childrens be F, A and B

Given that the ages of Father and his childrens A and B are

A = 3

B = 2

F = 40

Let us assume X be the required years then

According to problem Fathers age is twice that of Combined age of A and B after X years

i.e. (F+X) = 2×[(A+X)+(B+X)]

F+X = 2×(A+B)+(4×X)

40+X = 2×(3+2)+(4×X)

40-10 = 3×X

X = 30⁄3

X = 10

After 10 years te age of Father is twice that of the combined age of his childrens A and B
