Question :31

A is 2 years older than B who is twice as old as C. if sum of ages of A,B and C is 37 years. Find the age of A.

A.  7 years

B.  9 years

C.  14 years

D.  16 years

Answer: d

Let us assume that the age of C is X years

B is twice as old as C then

B = 2×X

A is two years older than B then

A=B+2

A = (2×X) + 2

Sum of their ages is 37

A+B+C = 37

{(2×X)+2}+(2×X)+X=37

5×X+2 = 37

5×X=35

X = 7

The age of A is

A = (2×X)+2

A = (2×7)+2

A=14+2

A=16
