Question :34

3 years ago, a fathers age was twice the sum of ages of his 4 sons. in 3 years time, the fathers age will be equal to sum of ages of the sons. Find the present age of father.

A.  24 years

B.  36 years

C.  39 years

D.  45 years


3 Years ago, let sum of age of four sons = x

Then fathers age (3 years ago ) = 2x

Fathers age (after 6 years ) = 2x+6

Sum of the ags of 4 sons (6 years hense ) = x + (6×4)

2x + 6 = x + 24

x = 18

Father present age = 2x + 3

= (2×18)+3

=39 years.

Alternative Method:

Let us assume the age of Father and his four sons be F, S1,S2,S3,S4

Fathers age is twice that of his four Sons before 3 years

i.e. (F-3) = 2×[(S1-3)+(S2-3)+(S3-3)+(S4-3)]

F-3 = 2×[S1+S2+S3+S4]-24

F+21 = 2×[S1+S2+S3+S4] --- > 1

And also given that Fathers age is equal to Sum of his four sons age after 3 years from present

Then

F+3 = [(S1+3)+(S2+3)+(S3+3)+(S4+3)]

F+3 = [S1+S2+S3+S4] + 12

F-9 = [S1+S2+S3+S4] --- > 2

From 1 and 2 we get

F+21 = 2×(F-9)

F+21 = 2×F - 18

F = 21+18

F = 39
