Question :15

2 years ago, the fathers age was 6 times of his sons age. 6 years hence the ratio between the ages of father and the son becomes 10:3. What is the fathers present age?

A.  40 years

B.  42 years

C.  44 years

D.  48 years

Answer:c

Let us assume that the present ages of Father and Son be F and S respectively

Given the fathers age is 6 times that of his Sons age before 2 years

(F-2) = 6×(S-2)

F = (6×S)-10--->1

And also Given that the ratio between fathers and his sons age becomes 10:3 after 6 years

i.e. F+6⁄S+6 = 10⁄3

(3×F)+18 = (10×S)+60

Substitute 1 in Above equation we get

3×((6×S)-10) = (10×S)+42

(18×S)-30 = (11×S)+42

8×S = 72

=> S = 9

From Equation 1

F = (6×S)-10

F = (6×9)-10

F = 54-10

F = 44.
