Question :95

A train travelling at 48 km/hr crosses another train, having half its length and travelling in opposite direction at 42 km/hr, in 12 seconds. It also passes a railway platform in 45 seconds. The length of the railway platform is:

A.  200 meters

B.  300 meter

C.  350 meters

D.  400 meters


Relative speeds of two trains = (48+42)

=90 km/hr

=90×5⁄18 m/s

= 25m/s

Total distance covered in 12 seconds 12×25=300 meters

Length of the first train= (300×2)÷3

=200 meters

Distance covered by the first train in 45 seconds

= 48×5⁄18 ×45

=600 meters Length of the platform = 600-200

=400 meters.
