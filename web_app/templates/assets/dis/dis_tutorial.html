Time and Distance

  1. Distance Covered = Time taken × Average speed.

  2. Time taken
    = Distance covered ⁄Average speed.

  3. Average Speed = Distance Covered ⁄Time Taken.

    Note: Speed of the object is deemed to be constant during the whole journey, unless stated otherwise.

  4. if the speed of two trains are in the ratio of x:y, then ratio of time taken by them to cover a certain distance

    = 1 ⁄x : 1 ⁄y.

    = y : x.Respectively.

  5. To cross a pole or a standing man, a train is required to cover distance equal to its own length.

  6. To cross a platform or any other stationary objects of some length, a train is required to cover distance equal to sum of its own length and the length of the platform or the object.

  7. 1 km per hour = 5 ⁄18 metre per second.
    • To convert speed of an object from km/h to m/s, multiply the given speed by 5 ⁄18.

  8. 1 metre per second = 18 ⁄5 km per hour.
    • To convert speed of an object from m/s to km/h, multiply the given speed by 18 ⁄5.

  9. Relative Speed: If two trains are moving in opposite directions(either towards each other or away from each other), their relative speed is equal sum of their speed.

  10. If two trains are moving in the same direction, their relative speed is equal to difference of their speed
