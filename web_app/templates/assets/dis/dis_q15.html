Question :15

The ratio between the rates of travelling of A and B is 2:3 and therefore A takes 10 minutes more than the time taken by B to reach a destination. If A had walked at double the speed, the time taken by him to reach the destination would have been.

A.  25 minutes

B.  15 minutes

C.  30 minutes

D.  20 minutes

Answer:b

Ratio of speeds = 2:3

Ratio of time taken = 3:2

Difference of time = (3-2)ratio

= 1 ratio = 10 minutes

As normal time = 30 minutes.

Time taken by A at double the speed = 30÷2

= 15 minutes.
