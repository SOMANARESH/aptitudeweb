Formulae on Time and Distance


  1. Two persons left from place A for B at the speed of x km/h and y km/h respectively. The first person (first one) reaches at place B and immediately returns for place A. how far from place B will both the persons meet?

    First Method: Let total distance be d km and distance between B and the point of meeting = m km.

    Distance between B and meeting point = Distance (i.e. AB) × Different between speeds ⁄ Sum of speeds.

    Second Method:
    Let ratio of two persons = x:y
    Then ratio of distance travelled by them = x:y
    Distance covered by first person = x ⁄ x + y × Total distance covered.
    Distance covered by second person = y ⁄ x + y × Total distance covered.
    Total distance covered is = AB + BA = 2AB.

  2. A train leaves a station at x km/h and another train leaves the same station after T hours at y km/h.

    they will meet after xT ⁄ y-x hours,

    and distance = xyT⁄ y-x.

  3. A train leaves station A for B and another train leaves station B for station A at the same time. The trains cross each other on their route and reach their destinations in m and n hours respectively after crossing each other. The ratio of speeds of two trans

    = √ n  : √ m   .

    Rule: Speeds of the first and second trains are in the ratio of square roots of time taken by the second and the first train in reaching their destination after crossing each other.

  4. If two trains travels a certain distance at the speed of a km/h, b km/h respectively and difference between time taken by two trains is t hours. Then distance between the starting point and the destination

    = a × b ⁄ a-b  × t.

    Distance = Multiplication of speeds ×Difference of Time taken ⁄ Difference of speeds.

  5. If a person goes from A to B at x km/h and returns at y km/h, then his average speed during the whole journey is

    = 2xy ⁄ x + y.

    Total distance covered

    = 2 x y ⁄ x + y × Time taken

    Distance from A to B

    = 2xy ⁄ x+y ×Time Taken × 1 ⁄ 2.

  6. A man covers 1⁄3rd of his journey at x km/, next 1⁄3rd of this journey at y km/h and remaining 1⁄3rd journey at z km/h, then his average speed during the journey is

    = 3 x y z ⁄ x y + y z + x z.

  7. If speeds of two trains are in the ratio x:y. the first train crosses a pole in a seconds and the second train in b seconds. The time taken by the two trains to cross each other completely is

    = ax + by ⁄x + y

    i.e. Sum of lengths of the trains ⁄ Relative speed of the train.
