LCM (Least common Multiple)

  • If we goes on writing the multiples of each of the numbers, then the number which is least and common in each number is called LCM(Least Common Multiple)
EX: If we consider LCM of numbers 2,3,6,8.

2=2 2,4,6,8,10,12,114,16,20,22,24,2,28,30,32,34,36,38,40,42,44

3= 3,6,9,12,15,18,21,24,27,30,33,36,39,42,45,48,51,54,57,60

6=6,12,18,24,30,36,42,48,54,60

8=8,16,24,32,40,48,56,64,72,80

When you look at all the factors of numbers, common factor in all the numbers is 24, so 24 is the LCM of 2,3,6,8.

Way to solve LCM: LCM of 22,49,28,56.

Write the numbers as the following

from the least prime number divide all the numbers i) If the number is divisible by prime number then write the quotient under it, else write the same number under it.


ii) Do the same procedure by using next prime number until getting all ones in the last.


iii) Now multiply each prime numbers which are used to divide numbers

2 × 2 × 2 × 7 × 7 × 11 = 8 × 49 × 11 = 4312

so, 4312 is the LCM of 22,49,28,56.

HCF (Highest Common Factors)

  • If you goes on writing the factors of number individually, then we will find a greater number which is common in each number.
    then the common number is called HCF.
    If you didn't find any common factor then HCF = 1.
Ex: HCF of 3,7,9,5.
3 = 1,3
7 = 1,7
9 = 1,3,9
5 = 1,5,
HCF = 1.

Way to find HCF:

Find the HCF of 108, 288, 360.

  1. Take two largest numbers (288, 360)

    divide highest one with lowest number



    if the remainder is not zero, then divide the previous with the remainder



    If you get zero remainder then divisor at previous is HCF of the two numbers.

    If you didn't get zero remainder do the same procedure further.

    Ex: 42,68.




    HCF = 2.

    Repeat the procedure with HCF of 1st two numbers and the largest of remaining numbers

    HFC of 72, 108.


    HCF of 108,288,360 = 36

LCM by Power method:

  • LCM of 22, 49, 28,56.

    Write the each number in terms of power of prime numbers


    22 = 2 1 × 11 1

    49 = 7 2


    28 = 2 2 × 7


    56 = 2 3 × 7

  • Let you choose one prime number which is common in every number and select highest power of prime number in the given numbers.

    Here 23 is the highest power of 2 in the given numbers.

    • Note: If any number doesn't have power of selected prime number, then the power for that prime number is treated as 0

    49 = 2 0 × 7 2

    Similarly do for all the common prime number in the numbers

    LCM = 2 3 × 72 × 11 1 = 4312

HCM by power method:

  • It is similar to LCM, but instead of selecting highest power of prime numbers here we are selecting lowest power of prime number.

    Note: If any numbers doesn't have common prime number then leave that common prime number the power of prime number in that number is zero.

    Ex: 18, 54, 42,68, 20


    18 = 2 1 × 3 2

    54 = 2 1 × 3 3

    42 = 2 1 × 3 1 × 7 1

    68 = 2 2 × 17 1

    20 = 2 2 × 5 1
Note: In case of two numbers the product of LCM and HCF is equal to product of two numbers

Ex: 9,21

LCM = 3 × 3 × 7

HCF = 3.

LCM × HCF = 63 × 3 = 189

Product of 9,21 = 9 × 21 = 189

Note: In case of fractional numbers

Now find the LCM of numbers L1 × a1⁄b1, L1 × a2⁄b2, L1 × a3⁄b3

Now find LCM of resultant numbers

After that divide result by LCM of b1,b2,b3 (L1) then we will get LCM of

a1⁄b1, a2⁄b2, a3⁄b3
