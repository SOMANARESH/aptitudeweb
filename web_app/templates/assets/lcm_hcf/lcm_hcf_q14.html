Question 14:

LCM of (16 − x2) and (x2+x − 6) is?

A.(x − 3)(x+3)(4 − x2)

B.(4 − x2)(x − 3);

C.(x − 2)(x+4)

D.None of these.

Answer:D

Given numbers (16 − x2) = 42 − x2

= (4 − x)(4 +x)

(x2 +x − 6) = x2 +3x − 2x − 6

= x(x+3) − 2(x+3)

= (x − 2)(x+3)

(4 − x)1 (4+x)1, (x − 2)1 (x+3)1

Since we don't know the value of x, So each term is taken as a different prime number

So LCM = product of highest powers of prime nos

LCM = (4 − x)1 (4+x)1 (x − 2)1 (x+3)1

These is no particular solution in the options matching LCM so answer is none of these.
