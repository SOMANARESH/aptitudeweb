Calendar

  • Odd Days:
      In a given period, the number of days more than the complete weeks are called odd days
      Simply, The remainder obtained when you divide the total number of days with 7 (Total days of a Week).
  • Months and Days:

      There are 12 months in a year.

      Jan − 31 (days), Feb − 28; 29 (for a leap year), Mar − 31, Apr − 30, May − 31,June − 30; July − 31; Aug − 31; Sept −30; Oct − 31; Nov −30; Dec − 31.

  • Ordinary Year

      A year which consists of 365 days.

      Consists of 52 weeks and 1 day.This additional day, is called an odd day

      One ordinary year have 1 odd day

      Examples: 1993,1997,2003,1777,etc...
  • Leap Year

      A year which consists of 366 days.

      Consists of 52 weeks and 2 day. (February has 29 days).

      One leap year have 2 odd days.

      Every year which is not a century year (i.e. which is not a multiple of 100) is a leap year if and only if it is completely divisible by 4.

      Every century year is a leap year if and only if it is completely divisible by 400 or is an integral multiple of 400 (i.e. the remainder ought to be 0).

      Examples:

      • 2000 is a leap year. 2000 is completely divisible by both 4 and 400.

      • 1996 is a leap year. 1996 is completely divisible by 4.

      • 1900 is not a leap. This century year is not divisible by 400 even though it is divisible by 4.

    • Number of odd days

      • 100 Years

          In a century - i.e. 100 years, there will be 24 leap years and 76 non-leap years. This means that there will be 24 x 2 + 76 x 1 = 124 odd days. Since, 7 odd days make a week, to find out the net odd days, divide 124 by 7. The remainder is 5. This is the number of odd days in a century.

      • 200 Years

          As we know for 100 years, there are 5 odd days.

          So for 200 years, there are 2 × 5 = 10 odd days

          So there are total of 3 odd days after dividing total days by 7

      • 300 Years

          As we know for 100 years, there are 5 odd days.

          So for 300 years, there are 3 × 5 = 15 odd days

          So there are total of 1 odd days after dividing total days by 7

      • 400 Years

          As we know for 100 years, there are 5 odd days.

          So for 400 years, there are 4 × 5 = 20 odd days

          But as we know there should be one more odd in a leap year when compared to normal.

          Since 400 year is leap year, we need to add one more day to total odd days.So total = 21

          So there are total of 0 odd days after dividing total days by 7

          So for every 400 years, we will get 0 days and calendar repeats the same.
