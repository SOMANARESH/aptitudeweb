Formulae for Percentage.


  1. If a person spends x% of his income. Then his savings is (100 - x)% of the income.

  2. If x% of a number is y%, then the number is :

    = y × 100 ⁄x

    Note: if y can be expressed as ax, then the number = a × 100.

  3. If x% of a number is equal to y of the second number
    Then ratio between first and second number

    = 1⁄x   : 1⁄y = y : x.

  4. Two numbers are in the ratio x:y if the first number is increased by a% and the second number is increased by b% then

    (a) Percentage increase in the sum of the numbers

    = ax + by⁄100   × 100⁄x + y
    = ax + by⁄x + y.

    (b) Percentage change in difference of the number

    = ax - by⁄x- y

  5. The product of two or more numbers is increased by x% if one of the numbers is increased by x%. if the product of two or more numbers is decreased by x% if one of the number is decreased by x%.

  6. If price of a commodity is changed from Rs:1 to Rs:x⁄y, then to maintain his expenditure, a consumer must buy y⁄x units of the commodity instead of 1 unit.

    (hint: x⁄y × y⁄x = 1.)

  7. If we have two numbers, one of them is increased by x% and the other is decreased by x% Then the product of the numbers will be decreased by

    = x 2 ⁄100 %.

    Note:
    This formula can be used if a number is increased by x% and then the resultant number is decreased by x% and vice versa.

  8. If out of two numbers first number is increased by x% and second number is increased by y%, then percent increase in the product of two numbers is

    = x + y + x y ⁄ 100.

    Note:
    Decrease in a number can be written as negative increase e.g. if the first number is decreased by x% then we can write it as (-x)% increase.

    Note: This formula can be used in numerous situations, such as

    - If a number is increased by x% and then the increased number is again increased by y%, then total increase in percentage

    = x + y + x y ⁄ 100

    - If a number is increased by x% and the other number is decreased by y%, then percent increase in the product of two numbers

    = x + (-y) + x (-y) ⁄ 100.

    = x - y - x y ⁄ 100

    Alternate Methods:

    Method1:
    1. Assume that the original numbers are 10 each.
    2. Find new number after adding/subtracting percent increase/decrease in original numbers.
    3. Find product of the new numbers.
    4. Deduct 100 from the product of the new numbers. Balance is net increase or decrease in the product of two numbers.

    Method2:
    1. Let both numbers are 10 each. Then new numbers are 11 and 12.

    Important to note:

    The increase in percentage / fraction value is always more than the decrease in percentage/fraction.

    Formula stated above can be used in numerous situations, such as:

    1. If a number is increased and then by how much the new number is to be decreased to get the original number and vice versa.
    2. Price of commodity is first increased and then by how much new price is to be reduced to restore to the original price and vice versa.
    3. If price of commodity is increased then by how much quantity purchased is to be reduced so that total expenditure remains constant and vice versa.
    4. If first number is more than the second number then by how much the second number is less than the first number and vice versa.
