Question :29

The total number of students in two schools A and B is 990. If the number of students of school A is decreased by 5% and the number of students of school B is increased by 14% the number of students of the two schools will become equal. The ratio of the number of students of school A and B is:

A.  5:6

B.  6:5

C.  115:84

D.  84:115


Let the number of students in School A and B be X and Y

Given that A+B = 990

When number of students of school A decreased by 5% and number of students of School B increased by 14%, number of students in both the schools are equal

Number of students in school

A = X-(5% of X)

Number of students in school

A = X-(5⁄100×X)

Number of students in school

A = X-(5X⁄100)

Number of students in school

A = (100X-5X)⁄100

Number of students in school

A = 95X⁄100

Number of students in school

B = Y+(14% of Y)

Number of students in school

B = Y+(14⁄100×Y)

Number of students in school

B = Y+14Y⁄100

Number of students in school

B = (100Y+14Y)⁄100

Number of students in school

B = 114Y⁄100

Number of students of both the schools are equal

i.e. 95X⁄100 = 114Y⁄100

95X = 114Y

X⁄Y = 114⁄95

X⁄Y = 6⁄5
