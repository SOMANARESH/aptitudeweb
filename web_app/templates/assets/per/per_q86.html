Question :86

Numerator of a fraction is increased by 12% and its denominator is decreased by 2%. If value of the fraction becomes 4⁄7, find the original fraction.

A.  1

B.  1⁄2

C.  1⁄4

D.  7⁄4


Let the original fraction is P⁄Q

Given that numerator is increased by 12%

i.e., P+(12% of P) = P + (12⁄100)P

Numerator = (100P+12P)⁄100

Numerator = 112P⁄100

And also Denominator decreased by 2%

i.e, Q-(2% of Q) = Q - (2⁄100)Q

Denominator = (100Q-2Q)⁄100

Denominator = 98Q⁄100

Now the fraction after changes is 4⁄7

Numerator⁄Denominator = 4⁄7

(112P⁄100)⁄(98Q⁄100) = 4⁄7

112P⁄100 = (4⁄7) ×98Q⁄100

112P⁄100 = (4×98Q)⁄(7×100)

112P⁄100 = 14Q⁄25)

P⁄Q = 14⁄25×100⁄112

P⁄Q = (14×100)⁄(25×112)

P⁄Q = 4⁄8

P⁄Q = 1⁄2
