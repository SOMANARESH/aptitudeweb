Question :23

If 50% of (X-Y) = 40% of (X+Y), then what percent of X is Y?

A.  10 1⁄9%

B.  11 1⁄9%

C.  13 1⁄9%

D.  21 1⁄9%


Given that 50% of (x-y) = 40% of (x+y)

50⁄100×(x-y) = 40⁄100×(x+y)

50X-50Y = 40X+40Y

50X-40X = 40Y+50Y

10X = 90Y

X⁄Y = 90⁄10

X⁄Y = 9⁄1

Y = (1⁄9)×X

Y = (1⁄9)×100⁄100×X

Y = (100⁄9)×X⁄100

Y = (111⁄9)×X⁄100

Y = 111⁄9% of X
