Question :91

In an examination, 70% students qualified from school A. there are 20% more students in school B than in school A and number of students qualified in the exam are 50% more than the students qualified from school A. what is the percentage of students qualified to the number of students appeared from school B?

A.  30%

B.  70%

C.  87.5

D.  78.5%


Let the number of students in school A be X

Number of students qualified from school A = 70%

Aq = (70⁄100)X

The number of students in school B is 20% more than in school A

Let number of students in school B be Y

So Y = X+(20% of X)

Y = X + (20⁄100)X

Y = (100X+20X)⁄100

Y = 120X⁄100

Given that number of students qualified from school B is 50% more than in school A

Bq = Aq + 50% of Aq

Bq = Aq + (50⁄100)(Aq)

Bq = Aq + (1⁄2)(Aq)

Bq = ((2+1)⁄2)(Aq)

Bq = (3⁄2)(Aq)

Bq = (3⁄2)×(70⁄100)X

Bq = ((3×70)⁄(2×100))X

Bq = (210⁄200)X

Bq = (105⁄100)X

Now we need to calculate percentage of number of students qualified to total number of students from school B

i.e., Bq⁄Y = [(105⁄100)X]÷[(120⁄100)X]

Bq⁄Y = 105⁄120

Bq⁄Y = 7⁄8

Bq⁄Y = 0.875

Required percentage is 87.5%
