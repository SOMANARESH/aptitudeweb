Question :85

If the numerator of a fraction is increased by 20% and its denominator by 25%, then the fraction so obtained is 3/5. What is the original fraction?

A.  3⁄5

B.  2⁄8

C.  5⁄8

D.  None of these

Answer: C

Let the original fraction is P⁄Q

Given that numerator is increased by 20%

i.e., P+(20% of P) = P + (20⁄100)P

Numerator = (100P+20P)⁄100

Numerator = 120P⁄100

Numerator = 6P⁄5

And also Denominator increased by 25%

i.e, Q+(25% of Q) = Q + (25⁄100)Q

Denominator = (100Q+25Q)⁄100

Denominator = 125Q⁄100

Denominator = 5Q⁄4

Now the fraction after changes is 3⁄5

Numerator⁄Denominator = 3⁄5

(6P⁄5)⁄(5Q⁄4) = 3⁄5

6P⁄5 = (3⁄5) ×5Q⁄4

6P⁄5 = (3×5Q)⁄(5×4)

6P⁄5 = 15Q⁄20

P⁄Q = 15⁄20×5⁄6

P⁄Q = (15×5)⁄(20×6)

P⁄Q = 75⁄120

P⁄Q = 5⁄8
