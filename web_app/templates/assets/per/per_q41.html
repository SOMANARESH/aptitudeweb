Question :41

On increasing price of an article by 20%, number of articles sold fell by 20%. What is effect of this on total sales of the article?

A.  4% decrease

B.  4% increase

C.  20% decrease

D.  No effect

Answer: A

Let the price of an article be X and number of articles be Y.

Now Total price for X articles

= X×Y-->(1)

Given that price of article increased by 20%

Now new price of article = X+20% of X

New Price of an article = X+ (20⁄100)×X

New Price of an article = X+ (20X⁄100)

New Price of an article = (100X+20X)⁄100

New Price of an article = 120X⁄100

Now new number of sales = Y-20% of Y

New number of sales = Y-(20⁄100)×Y

New number of sales = Y-20Y⁄100

New number of sales = (100Y-20Y)⁄100

New number of sales = 80Y⁄100

Now total price for those sales

= 120X⁄100×80Y⁄100

Now total price for those sales

= (120X×80Y)⁄(100×100

Now Total price for those sales

= (120X×80Y)⁄(100×100)

Total price for whole sales

= 9600XY⁄10000

Total price for whole sales

= 96XY⁄100--->(2)

From (1) and (2)

Effect on sales = New sales-old sales

Effect on sales = (96XY⁄100) - XY

Effect on sales =(96XY-100XY)⁄100

Effect on sales = -4XY⁄100

Total sales decreased by 4%
