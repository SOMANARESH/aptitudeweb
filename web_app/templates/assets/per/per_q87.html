If numerator of fraction is increased by 20% and the denominator is decreased by 5%, the value of the new fraction becomes 5⁄2 . The original fraction is:

A.  24⁄19

B.  3⁄18

C.  95⁄48

D.  48⁄95

Answer: C

Let the original fraction is P⁄Q

Given that numerator is increased by 20%

i.e., P+(20% of P) = P + (20⁄100)P

Numerator = (100P+20P)⁄100

Numerator = 120P⁄100

And also Denominator decreased by 5%

i.e, Q-(5% of Q) = Q - (5⁄100)Q

Denominator = (100Q-5Q)⁄100

Denominator = 95Q⁄100

Now the fraction after changes is 5⁄2

Numerator⁄Denominator = 5⁄2

(120P⁄100)⁄(95Q⁄100) = 5⁄2

120P⁄100 = (5⁄2) ×95Q⁄100

120P⁄100 = (5×95Q)⁄(2×100)

120P⁄100 = 475Q⁄200)

P⁄Q = 475⁄200×100⁄120

P⁄Q = (475×100)⁄(200×120)

P⁄Q = 475⁄240

P⁄Q = 95⁄48
