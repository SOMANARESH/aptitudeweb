The probability that a student will pass in mathematics is 3 ⁄ 5 and that of english is 1 ⁄ 3. If the probability that will pass in both mathematics and english is 1 ⁄ 8,then the probability that he will pass in at least one subject is

A.   87 ⁄ 120

B.   97 ⁄ 120

C.   112 ⁄ 120

D.   53 ⁄ 120

Answer: b



Given that theh probability of a student to pass in Mathematics is 3 ⁄ 5 and English is 1 ⁄ 3

P(M) = 3 ⁄ 5 and P(E) = 1 ⁄ 3

Given that the Probability to pass in both Mathematics and English is 1 ⁄ 8

P(M ^ E) = 1 ⁄ 8

Now as per the formulae,

P(M U E) represents the Probability that stadent can pass in any of the subject

So P(M U E) = P(M) + P(E) - P(M ^ E)

P(M U E) = (3 ⁄ 5) + (1 ⁄ 3 ) - (1 ⁄ 8)

P(M U E) = (72 + 40 - 15) ⁄ 120

P(M U E) = 97 ⁄ 120
