The ratio of number of officers and ladies in the two companies are 3:1 and 2:5 respectively. An individual is selected to be a chairperson of their association. The chance that this individual is selected from the 1st company is 2 ⁄ 3 then the probability that the chairperson will be officer is

A.   25 ⁄ 42

B.   7 ⁄ 42

C.   23 ⁄ 42

D.   11 ⁄ 43

Answer: a



Given that the chance that individual is selected from 1st company = 2 ⁄ 3

So the chance that individual is selected from 2nd company = 1 - (2 ⁄ 3) = 1 ⁄ 3

Now we need to find the probability that the selected person form each group is an officer is

P(O) = (Officers Ratio from 1st company × Selection of 1st company) + (Officers ratio in second company × Selection of 2nd company)

Officers ration in 1st company = (3 ⁄ (3+1)) = 3 ⁄ 4

Officers ratio in 2nd company = (2 ⁄ (2+5)) = 2 ⁄ 7

So P(O) = [(3 ⁄ 4) × (2 × 3)] + [(2 ⁄ 7 ) × (1 ⁄ 3)]

P(O) = (1 ⁄ 2) + (2 ⁄ 21)

P(O) = (21+4) ⁄ 42

P(O) = 25 ⁄ 42
