Question :12

Six boys and six girls sit in a row randomly. The probability that all the six girls sit together is

A.   1 ⁄ 132

B.   1 ⁄ 66

C.   1 ⁄ 1584

D.   3 ⁄ 22




Given that there are six boys and six girls in a row.

So we have total of 12 persons in a row.

So the number of ways of arranging them in a row is 12!

n(A) = 12!

Note: Since this is arrangement and Order is important.

The arrangement of n people by taking n at a time is

nPr = n! × (n-r)!

Here r = n
nPr = n! × (n-n)! = n!

But given that among the all six girls are grouping together.

So if you make six girls into a group(Assume like a person) and taking remainng boys into account, there are total of 7 peoples at a time.

So we can arrange 7 people in a row in 7! ways.

But the girls in the group can be arranged in 6! ways.

So total arrangements = 7! × 6! = n(E)

Finally the probability that all girls sit together is

P(E) = n(E) & n(A)

P(E) = (6! × 7!) ⁄ 12!

P(E) = (6! × 7!) ⁄ (12 × 11 × 10 × 9 × 8 × 7!)

P(E) = (6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1) ⁄ (12 × 11 × 10 × 9 × 8)

P(E) = 1 ⁄ 132
