Question :1

When a coin is tossed, the Probability of getting a head is

A.   1 ⁄ 2

B.   − 1 ⁄ 2

C.   0

D.   1

Answer: a



In case of coin, we have two outcomes.

i.e, Head and Tail.

If one coin is tossed,then we have a possibility of getting either head or Tail.

Total possibilities n(A)= {Head, Tail} = 2

Now the possibility of getting a head is = (Number of possibilities of getting head) ⁄ (Total number of possibilities)

P(Head) = n(Head) ⁄ n(A)

Here n(Head) = Number of possibilities of getting head = 1

So, P(Head) = 1 ⁄ 2
