i.e, n(A) = {11,12,13,14,15,16,21,22,23,- - - - , 56, 61,62,63,64,65,66}

Now in this case we need to find the number of possibilities in which the sum of numbers on two dice is equal to 9.

The possible outcomes for this condition are

n(SUM9)= {36,45,54,63} = 9

p(SUM9) = 4 ⁄ 36 = 1 ⁄ 9
