Definition:

Probability is the chance of occurance of an event.

Simply we can say Probability is about chances

Ex: If you the probability of Winning a game for India Vs West Indies at home ground is 90 %, then we can say India wins 90 matches out of 100 matches at home ground.

The Probability of an Event is calculated by

P(E) = (Number of occurances of an event) ⁄ (Total number of occurances)

Properties :

Let us assume P(E) is the Probability of an event,then

1.P(E) = n(E) ⁄ n(A) where n(E) is the Number of occurances of an event and n(A) is the total number of occurances

2.Probability should be ≤ 1 and > 0

i.e, 0 <   P(E)   ≤   1

3.If the Probability of non event is P(E|)

then P(E) + P(E|) = 1

P(E) = 1 - P(E|)

P(E|) = 1 - P(E)

P(A) × P(B)

4.If we have two events A and B, then the relation between Probability of A ∩ B and A ∪ B is

P(A U B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A ∩ B)

Here ∩ represents AND Operation satisfying both.

∪ represents OR operation satisfying any one.

Example:

Let us assume that n be the number of students who passed in English and m be the number of students who passed in Science and l be the number of students passed in both the english and science, then the number of students passed in any of the exams is k.

Then the relation between all is given by

n(E U S) = n(E) + n(S) - n(E ∩ S)
