Question :9

Three coins are tossed. The probability of getting neither 3 heads nor 3 tails is

A.   3 ⁄ 4

B.   2 ⁄ 3

C.   1 ⁄ 2

D.   1 ⁄ 3




When three coins are tossed, then we will have following possibilities

Total outcomes = n(A) = {HHH,HHT,HTH,THH,HTT,THT,TTH,TTT} = 23 = 8

Here it contains 3 Heads and 3 Tails.

But we need to find the number of possibilities not getting either 3 Heads or 3 Tails.

So the possible Outcomes are

n(3H| OR 3T|) = {HHT,HTH,THH,HTT,THT,TTH} = 6

Note:   Here 3H| represents not getting 3Heads

So the probability of this case is

P(3H| OR 3T|) = n(3H| OR 3T|) ⁄ n(A)

P(3H| OR 3T|) = 6 ⁄ 8

P(3H| OR 3T|) = 3 ⁄ 4
