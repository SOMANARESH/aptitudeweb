Question :8

two dies are thrown together. What is the probability that the sum of numbers on the two faces is divisible by 4 or 6

A.   1 ⁄ 3

B.   5 ⁄ 18

C.   4 ⁄ 18

D.   7 ⁄ 18




Given that two dice are rolled.

So there are total of 62 = 36 outcomes

n(A) = 36

Now in this problem we need to find the total number of possibilities in which the sum of two numbers on the dice is divisible by either 4 or 6.

The maximum sum obtained by adding two numbers on the two dice = 6+6 = 12

Then the total multiples of 4 or 6 are = {4,6,8,12}

Now we need to get the possibilities of getting sum on the two dice = 4

n(SUM4) = {13,22,31} = 3

Similarly, n(SUM6) = {15,24,33,42,51} = 5

n(SUM8) = {26,35,44,53,62} = 5

n(SUM12) = {66} = 1

So total number of possibilities of getting sum on the dice is wither 4 or 6 n(4OR6)= n(SUM4) + n(SUM6) + n(SUM8) + n(SUM12)

n(4OR6) = 3+5+5+1 = 14

Now the probability that sum of the numbers on the dice is either 4 or 6 = P(4OR6)

P(4OR6) = n(4OR6) ⁄ n(A)

P(4OR6) = 14 ⁄ 36

P(4OR6) = 7 ⁄ 18
