Question :13

Two fair dice are thrown. Given that the sum of the faces on the dice is less than or equal to 4.Find the Probability that only one dice shows two.

A.   1 ⁄ 4

B.   1 ⁄ 3

C.   2 ⁄ 3

D.   1 ⁄ 2




Given that two dice are rolled.

So total outcomes = n(A) = 62 = 36

and given that the sum of two numbers on the two dice is less than or equal to 4.

So the possible sum values = {2,3,4}

So the total combinations = {11,12,13,21,22,31} = 6

But we need to find the probability that only one dice shows 2

So the combinations = {12,21} = 2

So the Probability = 2 ⁄ 6 = 1 ⁄ 3
