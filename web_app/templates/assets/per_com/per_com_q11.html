Question 11 :

In a question paper, there are 4 MCQ's having 5 options each. in how many ways candidate will not get all four correct answers?

A.625

B.624

C.1024

D.1023

Answer: B

Solution:

Scenarios

  • Let’s take 2 numbers(1,2) two positions.

    combinations are {11, 12, 21, 22 } = 4 = 22

  • Now let's take 2(1, 2 ) numbers 3 positions.

    Combinations = {111, 112 ,121, 122, 211, 212, 221, 222} = 23

  • 3 numbers (1,2,3) 2 positions

    Combinations = {11,12,13,21,22,23,31,32,33} = 9 = 32

  • Like that 4 numbers and 3 positions

    Number of Combinations = 43 = 64

This is similar to Scenarios. In this case there are 4 MCQ’s and 5 options for each question.

But the 3 persons enter the journey means they started picking the train from next stop of starting.

It is just like 4 positions filled with 5 numbers.

So total number of ways = 54= 625.

Those combinations contains all wrong choices and exact one combination of all correct answers.

So total number of ways candidate will not get all four correct answers = 625 - 1 = 624
