Question 7 :

In how many ways can 4 Apples be distributed to 7 children if each child can get any number of Apples?

A.28

B.7 4

C.4 7

D.35


Solution:

From the given problem, each child can get any number of Apples means each child can get at most 4 Apples.

You can distribute any no. of apples (1,2,3,4) to each child.

So 7 children will get 1 or 2 or 3 or 4 apples.

  • Child 1 -- 1 apple
  • Child 1 -- 2 apples
  • Child 1 -- 3 Apples
  • Child 1 -- 4 Apples

    • Similarly for remaining child

  • So 1 apple distributed to 7 children
  • 2 Apples Distributed to 7 children
  • 3 Apples Distributed to 7 children
  • 4 Apples Distributed to 7 children

    • Total combinations = 7×7×7×7 = 74
