Question 6 :

A train is going from Bangalore to Chennai and makes 5 stops between these stations. 3 persons enter the train during the journey with 3 different tickets. How many different sets of tickets may they have had?

A.455

B.10

C.405

D.135


Solution:

For this we need find out the number of different tickets available from Starting to end.

But the 3 persons enter the journey means they started picking the train from next stop of starting.

The combinations should be from

{1-2, 1-3, 1-4, 1-5, 1-C, 2-3, 2-4, 2-5, 2-C, 3-4, 3-5, 3-C, 4-5, 4-C, 5-C } =15

So there are 15 distinct tickets.

The number of ways of selecting 3 different tickets from 15 tickets is given by

15 C 3 = 15! ⁄ 3! × (15 - 3)!

15 C 3 = 15×14×13×12! ⁄ 3! × 12!

15 C 3 = 15×14×13 ⁄ 3×2×1 = 455
