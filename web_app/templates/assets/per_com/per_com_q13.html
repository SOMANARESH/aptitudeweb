Question 13 :

In how many ways can 4 Indians, 3 Chinese and 2 Dutch be seated in a row such that all persons of the same nationality sit together?

A.126

B.576

C.4!×3!×2!

D.1728


Solution:

Here each group of people are treated as group.

So let’s assume ‘I’ represents Indians Group, ‘C’ represents Chinese Group and ‘D’ represents Dutch Group.

So the number of ways of arranging these groups is

{ICD, IDC, CID, CDI, DCI, DIC} = 6

The persons in a Indian group can be arranged in 4 P 4 ways( Arrangement of 4 persons for 4 positions by taking 4 persons at a time)

4 P 4 = 4! = 24

  • n P n = n! ⁄ (n-n)! = n! ⁄ 0! = n!

    • Similarly for Chinese = 3 P 3 = 3! = 6

    For Dutch Group = 2 P 2 = 2! = 2

    So the total number of ways of arrangements = 6 × 24 × 6 × 2 = 1728
